Find Healthier Alternatives

Problem it solves: People often struggle to identify whether a product is truly healthy or if there are better options.

How AI helps:

  • Compares the product's sugar, fat, and fiber levels against other products
  • Recommends similar items with less sugar or more whole grains
  • Helps users manage conditions like diabetes, obesity, or heart disease through smarter swaps

Mood-Based Recommendations

Problem it solves: Food choices often depend on how someone feels — tired, stressed, or craving comfort. Most nutrition tools ignore this emotional factor.

How AI helps:

  • Suggests products that improve energy (e.g., higher complex carbs) or calmness (e.g., lower sugar, more oats)
  • Uses sentiment or behavioral data (sleep, stress levels) to tailor recommendations
  • Encourages mindful eating that supports emotional well-being

Check Diet Compatibility

Problem it solves: Consumers following diets (vegan, gluten-free, keto, etc.) need to quickly verify if a product fits their restrictions.

How AI helps:

  • Automatically flags ingredients that violate diet rules (e.g., gluten in wheat, animal-derived vitamin D)
  • Saves time scanning ingredient lists manually
  • Builds trust by providing personalized "safe-to-eat" or "avoid" alerts

Eco & Sustainability Score

Problem it solves: Many shoppers want to eat sustainably but can't easily assess a product's environmental impact.

How AI helps:

  • Evaluates packaging recyclability, farming practices, and carbon footprint
  • Gives a sustainability rating (e.g., A–F) to inform eco-friendly decisions
  • Encourages brands to use greener ingredients or production methods
